<p><p>The oilfield chemicals and surfactants market is categorized into four main types based on their ionic properties: </p><p>1. **Anionic**: These have a negative charge, effective in emulsification and reducing surface tension.  </p><p>2. **Cationic**: Positively charged, useful for corrosion inhibition and biocidal applications.  </p><p>3. **Non-Ionic**: Without charge, they excel in stabilizing emulsions and are less sensitive to water quality.  </p><p>4. **Amphoteric**: These can switch charges and are versatile, suitable for various applications. Additionally, "Others" encompass specialty chemicals not classified in the main categories.</p></p>
